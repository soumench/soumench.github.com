♦ The letter v is used to represent many different sounds.
In Spain and West Bengal it is sometimes used to represent the english B sound. In English the letter V is used to represenmt a labiodental fricative, which is sort of somehow like F, but voiced.
♦ In Spanish and Hindi the letter V may also mean a bilabial approximant, which is a sound very like B. The difference is that in approsimant V the lips ALMOST touch each other, but not so hard as to make any turbulence.
♦ In Hindi the letter V is sometimes pronounced as English W. That is called a semivowel. It is basically the same thing as an "u".
♦ In Sanskrit the two Hindi pronounciations are okay. Most people make the V that is between two vowels and approximant,and the v that is after a consonant a semivowel.

Why phrasal verbs give headaches to students?


When students that don't know Phrasal Verbs try to translate sentences like "He was looking for the broom in the dark room.", they usually use their notes or textbook or a dictionary and find out that look means "see". Then they get all confused because there is no way you can see anything in a room with no lights.

The way to fix this is using better notes, or better dictionaries that explain that "look for" means "search".

There is no way that we can figurte out the meaning of "look for" by joining the meaning of "look" and the meaning of "for". That just does not work.

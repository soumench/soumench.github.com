10. Separable Verbs

Some of the phrasals that have an object can have the preposition before or after the object.

How do officers keep the morale of their soldiers up?

How do officers keep up the morale of their soldiers?

Keep this side of the box up.

Keep up this side of the box.

With other phrasals, the preposition cannot be moved.

He looks after his mother.

Important:

When the object of a separable verb is a pronoun, the separation is compulsory.

They keep it up.

If the verb is inseparable, there is never a separation, be it pronoun or no pronoun --

He looks after her.

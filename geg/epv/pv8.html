8. What is better, "call on" or "visit"?

English speakers use both, and you may use whichever one you want.

This is a very common situation, because many of the English phrasal verbs have a synonym that is not a phrasal. Here are some pairs :

call on = visit

count on = rely

get away = escape

let down = disappoint

make up = invent

pick on = tease

Most english speakers are taught in school that in formal and written English, the one-word version of these pairs is often better style than the two-word version. You do not need to worry about that rule, because all the verbs that you are taught here are polite enough to be used anywhere. Just use the one that you can remember.

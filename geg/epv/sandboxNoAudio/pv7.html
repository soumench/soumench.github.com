7. Some common Phrasal verbs

carry on → continue

pick on → to treat badly or unfairly

bear on → influence, affect

hold on → wait

pin on → blame

come of to happen as a result,to be descended from

get over → to recover from something

get through → to overcome, to endure, to finish

look around → to search for something

hold back → to hesitate to act or speak

Write off → to cancel

call off → to cancel, to withdraw

put off → to evade, to delay

lay off → to suspend ot terminate from work

live off → depend as a source of income or support

see off → to bid farewell, to say good bye

ask in → to request

check in → to register at a hotel, airport or hospital

break in → to forcibly enter

rush in → enter quickly

trade in → to exchange something

stay in → to not go out

figure out → discover the solution

roll out → to introduce, to include, to launch

hang out → socialize

phase out → to remove gradually

hand out → to distribute

stand out → to become noticeable

get up → to rise from bed

make up → invented, not true

look up to → to admire

back up → to give support

mock up → to make a model of something

mix up → to confuse

pick up → to choose

fall down → to be unsuccessful, fail, drop

cut down → reduced in scope or length

break down → stop working

let down → to disappoint

narrow down → to remove less important options

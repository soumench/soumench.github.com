English Phrasal Verbs

Some particles of Phrasal Verbs

Of:
(i) to indicate something missing
(ii) to indicate a small part of something
For:
(i) to show concern
(ii) to show attachment
(iii) to show the reason
Over:
(i) to cover something
(ii) to indicate that something is complete
(iii) to change or transfer a thing (iv) to indicate a higher position (v) to examine something carefully
through:
(i) to indicate that an action is finished
(ii) to state that an action had a beginning and now it ended
(iii) to indicate a difficult phase / process (iv) to show the transfer of idea or thought
Around:
(i) to evade or avoid
(ii) to idle away
(iii) to influence people
Back:
(i) to perform the desired action,
(ii) to go back in time,
(iii) to repeat an action,
(iv) to resist, or hold


Phrasal Verb Meaning
come of to happen as a result,to be descended from
get over to recover from something
get through to overcome, to endure, to finish
look around to search for something
hold back to hesitate to act or speak

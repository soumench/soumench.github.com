English Phrasal Verbs

How a Phrasal verb is formed

English People create Phrasal Verbs by combining two or three words from different categories of English Grammar.
This combination completely changes the meaning of the individial words.


The usual pattern to combine or form a phrasal verb is the following:
Example #1:
look after [ verb + preposition ]
Here "look" is a verb and "after" is a preposition.
But the meaning of "look after" is " to take care".

Example #2:
give in [ verb + particle ]
Here "give" is a verb and "in" is a "particle".
But the meaning of "give in" is " to surrender".

[Note: A preposition and a particle look similar but we use them differently.
(i) We use “preposition” (which shows a link or connection) in a “prepositional phrase.
(ii) The “particle” functions as a part of the verb. It changes the meaning of the verb to form a phrasal verb.]

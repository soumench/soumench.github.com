English Phrasal Verbs

Spirit of the prepositions and particles

On
usually tells us the following:
(i) to continue something,
(ii) to make contact with something,
(iii) to support something,
(iv) to see the result of effect of something,
(v) to grab something quickly,
(vi) to consider or treat something


Phrasal Verb Meaning
carry on continue
pick on to treat badly or unfairly
bear on influence, affect
hold on wait
pin on blame

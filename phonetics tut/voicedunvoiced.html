Voiced and Unvoiced Consonants

We can visually represent consonants taking the help of Spectrogram and Phonetics.
I have used Wasp and Praat.

dA dhA tA thA > at first the graphs will look identical. But upon meticulous inspection here are the following differences:
The consonants dA and dhA are "voiced" consonants.
That means our vocal cord buzzes and the buzzing sound can be felt and heard. Even if the buzzing sound is 20 dB lower, it can be heard!
In a sprctrogram, this buzz can be seen near the base frequncy of our vocal chord vibration. Mine showed a span of 75 Hz to 370 HZ in dA and dhA.
Also the volume curve / sound pressure level curve ( the yellow curve in Praat) is not completely down / closed.

But in the case of tA and thA, there are no voiced bars at the bottom part.
The yellow volume curve / sound pressure level curve is completely down. That means there is no sound output. The consonant tA and thA are blocking all the sound output from our mouth.
This is the typical characteristics of a consonant. They do not sound unless they are accompanied by a vowel or semi-vowel.

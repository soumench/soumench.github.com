What is an 'Object'?

In a computer program, an 'object' is like a furniture with many lockers in which we store some data.



How do we store data inside an 'object'?
In a furniture, each drawer has a label and all the labels are different. We have to say what we want to save, and in which drawer we want it saved. Then to recover it we ask for the contents of that drawer. In computer parlance, a drawer label is called a key, and its contents are called a value.

Let's say we want an 'object' and then we want to put the English translations of some Bengali words in it.
We tell the furniture, please put the word "dog" into the drawer that has label "kukur". After a while we ask the furniture, read to me what you have in the drawer 'kukur' and the furniture replies "dog".

Now "dog" is said to be a value, and the "kukur" that we use to retrieve the value is said to be a key.



What happens if we ask the furniture to put "cat" into the "biRAl" drawer, and there is no drawer with that label?

The furniture will create a new drawer labelled as "biRal" and then put the word "cat" inside it.



What happens if we ask the locker to put "cat" into the "biRAl" drawer, and there is already drawer with that label? Does the word "cat" get added to the end of what is already in the drawer, maybe?

The earlier contents of the "biRal" drawer will be emptied and the new word "cat" will be added to that "biRal" drawer.



What happens if we ask what did we put in the drawer the word "bhAluk", and there is no drawer of that name because we never put anything in it? Will the furniture maybe google for the translation of "bAluk" and tell us that it is "bear".

When we do that, the furniture will give us a special thing called undefined.



So if we ask a furniture for the value of it drawer "xxx" and it gives us 'undefined'; Does that mean that there is no drawer of that name?

Sometimes it does, but not always. The furniture gives us 'undefined' when we ask for the contents of a drawer that does not exist. But it also gives us 'undefined', if the drawer does exist and we put earler "undefined" inside that drawer. By the way, putting 'undefined' inside a drawer is usually a bad idea.



Can we tell the furniture to delete a drawer?

When we tell the furniture to delete a drawer, it obeys us like a good boy. It deletes the drawer and its contents.
